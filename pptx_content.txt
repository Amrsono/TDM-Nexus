ICAR Monthly review for budget movements, uplift/handback requests and change-management approval VOIS What is ICAR? ICAR = IT Capital Allocation Review A monthly finance process where budget movements are reviewed and, where required, approved. Why would I need it? Need to move funds between projects or  programmes ? ICAR is where you present the case before funds can be moved. Two options to consider Uplift Request : additional funds to deliver the project based on revised scope, demand or change. Handback Request : return budget you no longer need. Change-management entry ticket 1 Change needed Scope, demand or budget move 2 Portfolio-approved CR State = Pending Approval 3 ICAR review Review and approve movement Take a ‘Portfolio approved’ CR to ICAR. See ‘How to Create a Change Request’ for guidance.

The new CCS Helix release model consists of 2 main parts: The CCS Release go-live cadence will change from every 3 weeks to every 6 weeks, with an In-Life drop for PBIs (Bugs) in-between.   There will be a Long Running Branch (LRB) for larger projects which are developed in parallel to the 6 weekly cadence until fully developed, tested and ready for deployment; when they will move to the next regular release for final testing and deployment

Release Rags ||  Definitions 19 These are the criteria Release team use to typically use to determine the RAG for projects targeting a release Early Readiness / Check Point 0 Red – Likely to miss the release Not confirmed as targeting the release; the business requirements or the E2E HLD are not final; Application VROMs have not started; Low-Level designs will not start before RPM.  These candidates will moved out of this release to backlog, for the Delivery Manager to target a later release. Within the  P2D ADO Project : In the early stages of the project, t he  Portfolio Planning teams create an Epic to plan the investment and capacity. The Epic is where the allocated budget for the project and it’s forecast spend and actuals are tracked. If you cannot find your Epic or if there are multiple for your project, ask the PMO for support, do not create your own Features are then created as a children of the Epic The Feature is where BAs do their analysis, capture acceptance criteria, work on the  E2E  HLD etc. Without a parent Epic in P2D, Release Management will not have the data needed to manage and commit your Feature Once the target release is defined, this Feature is then moved to the Release Management ADO. Within the  Release Management ADO Project : Building on  the p revious point, once in the RM ADO, review your Feature, especially it’s State and the E2E HLD fields Set the Feature’s Target Release to the Release that you expect to deploy all your deliverables Create child  Delivery  work ite ms, one for each release required for your Feature For each  Delivery , complete all key fields, especially the State, Description, Target Release and Consumption Plan Now create child  Function(s)  within your  Deliveries  to capture application impacts, work or support. Where the domain for that  Function  has adopted the  Function  work item, the  Function  will automatically move to their ADO Project The Domain now takes over the ownership of this work item W here the domain has not adopted the  Function , it remains within the RM ADO Project Ownership remains with the TDM, who works with the domain to keep the data in-sync with the domain’s tracking system High level journey - Summary

5 How Digital Channels Differ from IT Digital:   Digital is a non-assisted channel that focuses on customer-facing experiences – everything  the  customer sees and interacts with online. IT:   IT is the technology backbone – the systems and infrastructure that keep Vodafone running. 41 What is the Importance of a Technical Launch?  The purpose of a technical launch is to ensure that the product or service is fully functional, stable, and ready from a technology and operational standpoint before it becomes available to customers.  It emphasizes validating system performance, integration points, infrastructure readiness, and support processes. The decision to conduct a technical launch prior to a commercial launch is a business consideration, typically based on the complexity of the project. Following deployment during the technical launch, the feature is implemented in the production environment but remains inaccessible to end customers; testing can be performed through designated links. Customer Journey Testing (CJT) may be executed after the technical launch. Any Priority 1 (P1) or Priority 2 (P2) defects identified during CJT must be resolved before proceeding to the commercial launch.

Customer Journey Testing (CJT) should be executed after the technical launch has been successfully deployed in the production environment but before the commercial launch. When to execute CJT ? Prior to enabling the feature for public access, ensuring the entire customer experience works as intended. Why this timing is important ? CJT validates the end-to-end customer journey in a real production environment. It helps identify any gaps or defects that were not captured during earlier testing phases. 43 When do we need to execute DVT (Deployment Verification Testing)?  Deployment Verification Testing (DVT) should be executed immediately after deployment in the production environment . It is a quality gate between deployment and customer-facing activities. When to execute DVT ? Once the feature is live in production and stable. Timely identification and resolution of defects help avoid any negative impact on customers.
